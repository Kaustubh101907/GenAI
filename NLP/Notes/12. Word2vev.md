## ЁЯОн What is a "Feature Representation"?

In Bag of Words or TF-IDF, a word's vector length was determined by your vocabulary size. If you had 50,000 words, your vector was 50,000 slots long.

**Word2Vec does things differently:** Every single word is squeezed into a fixed, dense vector (typically **300 dimensions** long). Each slot represents a hidden grammatical or contextual "feature" (like Gender, Royalty, Age, or Food).

### ЁЯТб The Conceptual Grid (How the AI thinks):

| Vocabulary Words | Feature 1: Gender | Feature 2: Royalty | Feature 3: Age | Feature 4: Food |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Boy** | $-1.0$ | $0.01$ | $0.03$ | $0.05$ |
| **Girl** | $+1.0$ | $0.02$ | $0.02$ | $0.04$ |
| **King** | $-0.92$ | $+0.95$ | $0.75$ | $0.01$ |
| **Queen** | $+0.93$ | $+0.97$ | $0.68$ | $0.02$ |
| **Apple** | $0.01$ | $0.02$ | $0.85$ | $+0.91$ |

> **Why this fixes the math:** Notice how **King** and **Queen** score super high on the "Royalty" feature, while **Apple** scores super high on the "Food" feature. Because they are dense decimals instead of sparse 1s and 0s, there is no wasted memory!

## ЁЯзЩтАНтЩВя╕П Word2Vec Magic: Vector Arithmetic

Because these vectors represent true meaning, you can literally do **math equations with words**, and the answers make perfect sense!

$$\text{Vector}(\text{"King"}) - \text{Vector}(\text{"Man"}) + \text{Vector}(\text{"Woman"}) = \text{Vector}(\text{"Queen"})$$

$$\text{Vector}(\text{"King"}) - \text{Vector}(\text{"Boy"}) + \text{Vector}(\text{"Girl"}) = \text{Vector}(\text{"Queen"})$$

If you take a King, subtract the "male/boy" attributes, and add the "female/girl" attributes, the closest coordinate on the AI's map will land directly on **Queen**. ЁЯдп

## ЁЯУР The Math Rule: Cosine Similarity & Distance

To figure out how close two words are, the AI plots them on a graph and measures the **angle ($\theta$)** between their vector arrows. ЁЯП╣

### 1. Cosine Similarity (The Angle)

It calculates the cosine of the angle between two vectors:

* $\cos(0^\circ) = 1$ тЮбя╕П The arrows point in the **exact same direction** (Identical meaning).
* $\cos(90^\circ) = 0$ тЮбя╕П The arrows are perpendicular (Absolutely **zero relationship**, like *King* and *Apple*).
* $\cos(180^\circ) = -1$ тЮбя╕П The arrows point in **opposite directions** (Exact antonyms).

### 2. Cosine Distance (The Final Score)

To convert this angle into a clean physical distance score, we use the formula:


$$\text{Distance} = 1 - \text{Cosine Similarity}$$

* **If words are identical:** Similarity = $1 \implies \text{Distance} = 1 - 1 =$ **`0`** (No distance between them!).
* **If words are completely unrelated:** Similarity = $0 \implies \text{Distance} = 1 - 0 =$ **`1`** (Maximum distance).
* **Your calculation in the video:** A $45^\circ$ angle yields a similarity of `0.707`, making the distance `0.29`. Because `0.29` is very close to `0`, the AI knows the words are highly related!

## ЁЯОм Real-World Use Case: Recommendation Systems

This exact math is how Netflix or Spotify recommends things to you!

* Instead of words, the vocabulary list consists of **Movies** (e.g., *Avengers*, *Iron Man*).
* Instead of language features, the columns are movie genres (**Action**, **Comic Book**, **Comedy**).
* Because *Avengers* and *Iron Man* have nearly identical feature scores, their mathematical distance is almost **0**. When you finish watching *Avengers*, the AI checks its map, finds the closest neighbor, and recommends *Iron Man*! ЁЯН┐
