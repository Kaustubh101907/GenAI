### 39. Stemming 🪓

* **What it is:** A quick, rough-and-tough way to chop words down to their base root. It acts like a blind meat cleaver—it just cuts off the endings of words without checking a dictionary.
* **Example:** "Crying", "cries", and "cried" all get chopped down to **"cri"**. (It doesn't care that "cri" isn't a real English word!).

### 40. Lemmatization 🧠

* **What it is:** The smart, sophisticated cousin of Stemming. It uses a real dictionary and understands grammar rules to find the *true* root meaning of a word (called a "lemma").
* **Example:** "Changing" becomes **"change"**, and "better" becomes **"good"**. It *always* leaves you with a real, meaningful word.

### 41. Stopwords 🗑️

* **What it is:** Throwing the "filler" words into the trash. These are common words that don't give the AI any useful clues about the sentence's actual topic.
* **Example:** Removing words like *“is”, “the”, “an”, “at”*. If a sentence is "The pizza is great", the AI throws away "The" and "is", leaving just **"pizza, great"**.

### 42. Parts of Speech (POS) Tagging 🏷️

* **What it is:** Giving every word a grammar sticky-note. It reads your sentence and labels whether a word is a Noun, Verb, Adjective, etc.
* **Example:** In the sentence "Taj Mahal is beautiful", it tags *Taj Mahal* as a **Proper Noun** and *beautiful* as an **Adjective**.

### 43. Named Entity Recognition (NER) 🌍

* **What it is:** The ultimate detector. It goes beyond grammar and looks for real-world "things" like names of specific people, geographic places, dates, or companies.
* **Example:** In your earlier code, it looked at "Eiffel tower" and "Naithani" and accurately tagged them as a **Location** and a **Person**.
